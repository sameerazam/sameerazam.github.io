Capitalism

Capitalism is an economic system based on the private ownership of the means of production and their operation for profit.
Critics of capitalism argue that it concentrates power in the hands of a minority capitalist class that exists through the exploitation of the majority working class and their labor, prioritizes profit over social good, natural resources and the environment, is an engine of inequality, corruption and economic instabilities, and that many are not able to access its purported benefits and freedoms, such as freely investing.
Many theories like Centralisation Theory condemn the way how Capitalism is centralising Money and Power to a small fraction of people leaving behind a large unemployed population. Many critics believe that poverty in some parts of the world is a result of exploitation of their resources and opportunities by others due to Capitalism.

Al Jazeera's Ali Rae mentioned some of the drawbacks of capitalism in her Episode 1 of All Hail the Lockdown.

    

History of Capitalism

Capitalism has its roots in Colonisation and was carried across the world by broader processes of globalization and by the beginning of the nineteenth century a series of loosely connected market systems had come together as a relatively integrated global system, in turn intensifying processes of economic and other globalization.

What is wrong with Capitalism?

Capitalism is a chain connected system, if one person do something wrong, all of the system is affected. One such example is the Recession of 2008 where all of the markets were down mainly because of the fraudlent by some people.

Some of the short comings of Capitalism are;
  • Social and economic inequality
  • Unfair distribution of wealth and power
  • Materialism
  • Repression of workers and trade unionists
  • Social alienation
  • Unemployment
  • Economic instability
